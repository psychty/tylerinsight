About Me

There are three loves in my life, my family, my work, and black coffee [an unordered list].

I am currently a Public Health Analyst working for West Sussex County Council, I have a strong passion for learning new data visualisation methods and am particularly interested in interactive visualisation and story telling.

Please get in touch, to discuss any work presented here or to discuss your own data analysis requirements. I work full time and have a toddler so my spare time is largley taken up by sleep, but I am keen to share my data analysis skills with others where I can.

I am particularly interested in supporting small, not-for-profit, organisations to make best use of their own data, or relevant publicly available data to enhance decision making.

Portfolio

These are some key projects which use publically available data to explore populations and their health outcomes and behaviours. Some are static infographics, and some are interactive web pages. Many of these use a combination of R scripts and html/javascript libraries; notably the d3 javascript library. Some projects link to published standalone webpages or pages within my organisations' Joint Strategic Needs Assessment site and others link to GitHub repositories of code.

I make no claims to be an expert in these technologies, particularly javascript and web development, and I am constantly learning new methods. There are plenty of different methods to achieve similar outcomes and as time goes by I learn more efficient methods to achieve more with less [code]. I have tried to annotate code where possible to explain the processes in each of the scripts, from data extraction and analysis to exporting data and visualising patterns.

One of the beauties of open source languages such as R and d3 is that there is a wealth of community support from fellow analysts and developers to overcome challenges in presenting results and I have learned much from others sharing their work (see my 'data heroes' section to find wiser researchers and designers who have inspired my work). I hope the projects detailed here, in turn, contribute in some small way to the passionate community of data scientists and researchers in the field of public health and healthcare intelligence.

This is a work in progress, like many of my github repos.

In the meantime please visit my Github repo to see some of the projects I have been working on.
